Feature Prompt: Identity Archetypes + Rare Encounters

Enhance SOLSTICE VIGIL with two systems designed to encourage long-term play and sharing.

1. Archetype System

The game should gradually reveal what kind of wanderer the player is becoming.

Do not treat these as classes chosen at the beginning.

Instead, infer them naturally from hundreds of decisions.

After enough cycles, the player earns titles and identities based on their tendencies.

Examples:

Light-aligned

* Sun-Walker
* Ember Saint
* Dawnkeeper
* Flame Herald

Night-aligned

* Night-Walker
* Dream Shepherd
* Moon Herald
* Keeper of Echoes

Balanced

* Twilight Pilgrim
* Wheelkeeper
* Threshold Walker
* Warden of the Turning

Rules

* Archetypes are discovered, not selected.
* Players can drift between identities over time.
* Transformation is never permanent.
* Titles should feel poetic and mysterious.
* NPCs and narration should react differently depending on the current archetype.
* The game should occasionally acknowledge previous identities.

Examples:

“The ferryman recognizes traces of the Ember Saint you once became.”

“Though you wear the name Dream Shepherd now, the warmth of older days still clings to you.”

Shareability

Every few cycles, present a beautiful card:

Cycle 37
You have become a Dream Shepherd.

or

Cycle 112
The world now knows you as the Dawnkeeper.

These moments should feel rare and worthy of screenshots.

2. Rare Encounter System

Introduce extremely uncommon locations, characters, and events.

Players should feel that they are discovering things others may never see.

Examples:

Characters

* The Clockmaker
* The Last Bird
* The Sleepless King
* The Cartographer of Forgotten Paths
* The Child Beneath the Ice

Places

* The Frozen Observatory
* The Garden of Unspoken Names
* The Library Without Shadows
* The Sea of Ashen Mirrors
* The Tower at the Edge of Noon

Events

* Lunar eclipse
* Falling stars
* A day where the wind speaks
* A bridge that only exists once every hundred cycles
* The return of someone abandoned long ago

Rules

* Rare encounters should be genuinely uncommon.
* Some should require specific archetypes.
* Some should require balanced alignment.
* Some should require previous choices from dozens of cycles earlier.
* Encounters should reference the player’s history.

Example:

“The Clockmaker remembers that eighty-three vigils ago you refused his bargain.”

⸻

Discovery Tracking

Maintain a hidden codex of discovered wonders.

When something truly rare happens, show messages such as:

First encounter with The Clockmaker.

A phenomenon seen by very few wanderers.

or

The Tower at the Edge of Noon has appeared.

These moments should create feelings of mystery and awe rather than completion.

⸻

Design Philosophy

SOLSTICE VIGIL is not about winning.

It is about identity, memory, transformation, and wonder.

Players should frequently think:

* “I wonder what I might become.”
* “I wonder if anyone else has seen this.”
* “I can’t believe the game remembered that.”

The vigil turns.

The wheel does not stop.
