SOLSTICE VIGIL is a solo, narrative RPG about maintaining balance in a world where time has broken.

At the June solstice, the sun stopped setting. Day and night no longer alternate naturally. A lone wanderer—the player—has taken on an endless duty: keeping the wheel of light and darkness turning.

Core premise

You travel through strange places frozen between extremes:

* Bridges of halted sunlight.
* Silent valleys where night waits.
* Guardians transformed by imbalance.
* Forgotten shrines built to sustain the cycle.

There is no final boss and no ending. The choices you made decide how many days you'll be vigilant

Main mechanic

You constantly balance two forces:

☀️ Long Day

* Warmth
* Action
* Passion
* Pride
* Memory

🌙 The Hush

* Stillness
* Reflection
* Forgetting
* Solitude
* Dreams

Every choice pushes you slightly toward one side.

If you reach an end your journey ends. You can see how many days and share it. 


Eventually you may drift back toward balance, or embrace one side again.

Gameplay loop

1. Arrive at a scene.
2. Read atmospheric narration.
3. Choose among three responses:
    * Embrace the light.
    * Embrace the dark.
    * Remain between.
4. Adjust the balance meter.
5. Encounter new places, characters, and mysteries.
6. Record another cycle.
7. Continue forever.

Themes

The game is about:

* Balance instead of victory.
* Transformation instead of death.
* Duty without completion.
* Identity and change.
* Accepting cycles rather than escaping them.

Inspiration

It sits somewhere between:

* Journey
* Sunless Sea
* 80 Days
* The Longing
* Book of Hours
* Kentucky Route Zero
* Solo journaling RPGs like Thousand Year Old Vampire

The central idea can be summarized as:

The world survives because someone keeps walking.

You are that someone.

The vigil turns. The wheel does not stop.
